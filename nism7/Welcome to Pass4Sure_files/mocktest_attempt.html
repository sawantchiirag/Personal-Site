Q 1. The 'Cash Obligations' related to transactions executed on a stock exchange are settled by _______ .
Depository Participants
Clearing Banks
Stock Exchanges

  CORRECT ANSWER
   WRONG ANSWER
CORRECT ANSWER:

Clearing Banks

Explanation:
Clearing Bank(s) acts as an important intermediary between a clearing member(s) and the clearing corporation. Every clearing member needs to maintain an account with a clearing bank. Its the function of the clearing members to ensure that the funds are available in his account with clearing bank on the day of pay-in to meet the obligations.
 
All transactions of pay-in/pay-out of funds are carried out by these clearing banks. The obligation details are passed on to the clearing banks, who then carry out the pay-in/payout of funds based on the net obligations.

Q 22. Whenever there is a market trend in either direction, the dynamic price bands shall be relaxed by the stock exchanges in increments of ___ at a time.
2%
5%
10%
7.5%

  CORRECT ANSWER
   WRONG ANSWER
CORRECT ANSWER:

5%

Explanation:

For scrips on which no price bands are applicable, Stock Exchanges have implemented a mechanism of dynamic price bands. This mechanism prevents the acceptance of orders for execution that are placed beyond the price limits set by the Stock Exchanges. Stock Exchange shall set the dynamic price bands at 10 percent for such securities.

In the event of a market trend in either direction, the dynamic price bands shall be relaxed by the Stock Exchanges in increments of 5 percent at a time in the direction of price movement during the day.

Q 35. Identify the correct statement with respect to adjustment of dividend in the Futures and Options (F&O) segment.
(A) The dividend percentage is multiplied in the open positions (B) Adjustments of dividend is done for all dividends.
Only A is correct
Only B is correct
Both A and B are correct
Neither A nor B is correct

  CORRECT ANSWER
   WRONG ANSWER
CORRECT ANSWER:

Neither A nor B is correct

Explanation:

For the purpose of adjustments in equity F&O, dividends which are below 2 percent of the market value of the underlying stock, would be deemed to be ordinary dividends and no adjustment in Equity F&O would be made for ordinary dividends.

In case of declaration of "extra-ordinary" dividend by any company, the total dividend amount would be reduced from all the strike prices of the option contracts on that stock. The revised strike prices would be applicable from the ex-dividend date specified by the Exchange. All open positions shall be carried forward at the daily settlement price less dividend amount for the respective futures contract.
